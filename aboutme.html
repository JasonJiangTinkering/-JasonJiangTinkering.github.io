A path I could follow

Born in Brooklyn NY

I was born in Brooklyn, NY as the first Gen of my family to be born in the US. My parents were both teachers, manderian teachers. They both were overqualified having earned their computer science degrees at City College in NY. But the computer sciense field was not as inviting to them as they had hoped.

I was raised in a household that valued education and hard work. In kindergarten, I was already in a gifted program. In 5th grade, moved to another gifted school. Took computer science APs in Middle School, studied for 3 years to get into the top high school in the city. I played violin, had few friends, but wasn't bullied. The flow was easy. I knew how to study, and I was content living the same day. The plan was simple, I would do well in school, get into a good college, and get a good job.

Be the computer scientist - with the life my parents couldn't have navigated.

Jason sees the world

but what will he do

In high school, the pressure was off. My parents were scared of the schools reputation of overwork and they wanted me to make all the choices from now on. I was taking the subway, every day on my own. My highschool was massive, and with 70% of the students being Asian, I was among people who look, spoke, and had similar lives as me as I walked around. Just like before, I would do all the things I saw before me. I joined the fencing team, I took all the APs I could, I hanged out with the girls I liked and the guys I wanted to be like. Suddenly, I really liked a lot of things. Each day was just as full as before, but I felt each day kept being really special.

Follow the Want

I want to be good at my job

The captains of my fencing teams, the computer science teachers with interesting projects, the math team students who are geniuses in my classes. My hackathon team that I won 4 hackathons with.

I wanted to be like them, to stay productive. It was effortless to find the motivation to work hard, the days and nights away. I would finish all my class work in the day, do fencing through the night. Sleep and repeat.

Freedom to Responsibility

During Sophmore year, I was chosen to be captain of the fencing teams.

Faithfully leading by example, I was able to focus on my skills in fencing, leading my team to city wide champs.

In college, with a lack of fencing, I jumped on startup teams. Guiding decisions at Ermg, taking role in development startup events and coaching students.

I was able to take the freedom I had to do what I wanted, and turn it into a responsibility to lead others.

But what's the point

I was having a conversation with Celine Krzan.

When a entrapneourship professor who has taught me so many intersting ways to approach startups I thought I would learn how to do my job better. But she asked me how long could I could see myself working on my startup idea?

I couldn't. I had been working on my startup idea for a year, and I had been doing it because the process was fun, but the result left me no more furfilled. What I was doing, was missing a level of depth.

Follow the Humanity

Non-profit work

I spent the summer of 2023 working at the Tool Library, a non-profit in Buffalo. Everyday they were open, I would be in the front desk.

I was productive and they appreciated how proud I was of my work. But they also just wanted me as a person to stay as friends.

I had friends. A community of people who I spent time with. With a shared mission to borrow tools and build community.

During Sophmore year, I was chosen to be captain of the fencing teams.

Faithfully leading by example, I was able to focus on my skills in fencing, leading my team to city wide champs.

Human resources

where they fail us

Keiah Shauku, a friend of mine, told me that, that industry tries to turn people into bad robots. To push them to do a task, changing the person who was there before. And we can't do that. First because people who learn to be bad robots, will struggle when the task is different. Second because humans are valuable.

Moral of the story
  1. I was doing what I was doing because I was good at it
  2. Then I did what I did, because I liked getting good at it and being liked by people.
  3. I strive to do was do things because these people are my friends, and the work I do affects their lives.

So I can find peace. So there is a point of life, besides work. And most importantly I feel like human, and not the bad robot work that doesn't fill my bucket.
